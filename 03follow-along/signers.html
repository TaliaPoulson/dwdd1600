Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Several signers had major influence during and after the Revolution. Here are key figures:

Thomas Jefferson
Primary drafter of the Declaration. His words on equality and liberty shaped American democracy. Delegate from Virginia.
John Adams
Led the push for independence in Congress. Later became the second U.S. president. Delegate from Massachusetts.
Benjamin Franklin
Edited the Declaration and won French support through diplomacy, which was critical to victory. Delegate from Pennsylvania.
John Hancock
First to sign and president of the Continental Congress. His large signature became a symbol of defiance. Delegate from Massachusetts.
Samuel Adams
Organizer of colonial resistance, including the Boston Tea Party. Helped unify the colonies for independence. Delegate from Massachusetts.

The most influential signers of the Declaration of Independence helped define the nation's core values. Through their leadership, writing, and courage, they turned revolutionary ideas into a lasting government. Their actions continue to shape how Americans view freedom, equality, and the responsibilities of citizenship today.
